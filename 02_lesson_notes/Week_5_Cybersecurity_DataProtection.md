## Session 9: Introduction to Cybersecurity

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this session, students will be able to:
1. Define cybersecurity and explain why it matters in 2026.
2. Identify common cyber threats (Phishing, Ransomware, Social Engineering).
3. Explain the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability).

### Core Concepts

**What is Cybersecurity?**
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. It is the digital equivalent of locking your doors and installing security cameras for your data.

**Top 3 Cyber Threats in 2026**
1. **Phishing:** Fake emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages designed to steal your passwords. This is the #1 threat in Nigeria, especially targeting banking apps and USSD codes.
2. **Ransomware:** Malicious software that locks your files and demands money (often in cryptocurrency) to unlock them.
3. **Social Engineering:** When a hacker manipulates a human (not a computer) into giving up confidential information. Example: Calling an employee pretending to be IT support to ask for their password.

**The CIA Triad (The Golden Rule of Security)**
- **Confidentiality:** Only authorized people can see the data.
- **Integrity:** The data has not been tampered with or altered.
- **Availability:** The data is accessible when the user needs it.

### Practical Activity
- Display a **fake phishing email** on the projector.
- Ask students to spot 4 red flags: 
  - Wrong sender email address.
  - Spelling and grammar mistakes.
  - Urgent threat or request for action.
  - Suspicious links (hover over the link to see the true destination).

### Homework Questions
1. What is cybersecurity, and why is it important for everyday internet users?
2. Name two common cyber threats and explain how they work.
3. What does the "C" in the CIA Triad stand for, and why is it important?

## Session 10: Data Protection & Privacy

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this session, students will be able to:
1. Understand what data encryption is.
2. Apply the 3-2-1 Backup Rule.
3. Know the basics of Nigeria’s NDPR data protection law.

### Core Concepts

**What is Encryption?**
Encryption is the process of scrambling data into an unreadable format. Even if a hacker steals your file, they cannot read it without a special "key" to unlock it. Think of it as locking your digital files inside a vault.

**The 3-2-1 Backup Rule** (A must-know for every IT professional)
- **3** copies of your important data.
- **2** different types of storage media (e.g., external hard drive + USB flash drive).
- **1** copy stored offsite (e.g., Google Drive, iCloud, or an offsite server).

**Nigeria’s NDPR (Nigeria Data Protection Regulation)**
The NDPR is a Nigerian law that protects the personal data of citizens. It requires companies and organizations to:
- Ask for clear, explicit consent before collecting your data.
- Inform you how your data will be used.
- Keep your data secure and private.

### Practical Activity
- Show students how to create a **password-protected ZIP folder** (encryption).
- Show them how to back up that folder to **Google Drive** (offsite storage).

### Homework Questions
1. What is data encryption, and why is it important for online banking?
2. Explain the 3-2-1 Backup Rule in your own words.
3. What is the NDPR, and how does it protect you as a Nigerian citizen?
